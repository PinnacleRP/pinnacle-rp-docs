# 🏴 Racing Crew Rules

Here will be all of the Racing Crew Rules, if there is a rule not specifically stated in this, you are to go off of the overall gang rules. Any questions, please open a ticket.

## Hostile Rules

Racing crews are NON hostile crews. This includes, but not limited to:
- Starting beef with gangs (shootout, trotting, etc)
- Participating in heists as anything other than the getaway driver
- Producing drugs
- Any KOS

The following are allowed to be done NOT in your crew colors:
- Producing drugs
- Participating in heists as a non-getaway driver

If you are pressed at any hostile zone with your crew colors not on, you are not to retaliate as/with your racing crew.

**Crew Baiting (Prohibited)**

Crew baiting—intentionally provoking another gang without legitimate roleplay justification—is not allowed. This includes, but is not limited to:
- Spray painting another crew’s compound or turf without RP justification
- Posting inflammatory or provocative messages on Birdy without roleplay reasoning
- Spreading false rumors about a gang with no storyline or RP purpose

Engaging in crew baiting may result in disciplinary action.

**Multi-Crew Engagement & Handholding Policy**

Multi-Crew engagement and Handholding is prohibited for Racing Crews. As there should be 0 hostility in the scene.


# Crew Structure

## Non-Tagged Members

Non-tagged members are individuals who associate with a crew but are not officially listed on the roster.

Rights and privileges:

- Participate in basic RP interactions with the crew.
- Can not host racing events
- Can not participate in crew racing events WITH colors on
- Wear crew clothing anytime outside of Races or Racing Events

Restrictions:

- Cannot participate in crew races, pinkslips or getaway driving (Baby Driver app included)
- Cannot represent the crew in official matters (e.g., races and events).
- Cannot carry or use the crew's stash or equipment.

Any non tagged member WEARING crew clothing breaking any rules, may result in a gang strike/punishment

## Tagged Members

Tagged members are officially listed on the crew roster and hold full crew privileges:

- Represent the crew in all aspects of racing crew events, races, etc.
- Access gang resources (e.g., stash, turf, sprays).
- Participate in declared races and crew-specific events.
- Wear crew clothing at all times.

It is the leader's responsibility to ensure that all tagged members are aware of the rules and consequences related to representing the crew.

## Tagging Cooldowns

To prevent roster abuse, the following cooldowns apply:
- Returning after Soft PK via Hostile Patch Over Loss: 3-day cooldown.
- Returning after Soft PK or Crew Disbandment: 7-day cooldown.

Note: Cooldowns are server-wide and may only be waived with Gang Staff approval.

## Roster Rules

- Once a Crew vs Crew race is declared, rosters are locked until the conflict ends.
- Removed members have a 10-day cooldown before rejoining the same crew.
- Roster exploitation (dropping and re-adding members to gain an advantage) is prohibited.

## Racing Patch Over

A Racing Patch Over occurs when a crew acquires a member from another crew. This can be permitted, but must be supported by strong roleplay and meaningful conflict.

Requirements:
- Approval must be obtained from gang management before initiating a racing patch over.
- The crew losing the member in the patch over may respond with a series of races toward the acquiring crew, resulting in ongoing in-character conflict.

Mandatory Patch-Over period:

The following has to happen in order:
- Best of 3, 5, or 7 Rounds of Racing between the two crews: Decided by the organization losing the member

- If the crew acquiring the member loses: Immediate Soft PK of the patched member. The member must wait 3 days before joining another gang.
- If the crew acquiring the member wins: The patched member joins immediately with no cooldown or pk

Once the racing period ends, the conflict is considered resolved, regardless of the outcome.

## Soft PK vs Hard PK

**Soft PK (Partial Death):**

- You forget the events of the scenario and lose all memory related to the interaction.
- You forget everything related to the gang (members, stash, turf, etc.).
- You may remember that you were once part of the gang but nothing more.
- You cannot seek revenge or retaliate based on the event.

**Hard PK (Full Death):**

- Your character is permanently lost, and you have no further ties to the gang or storyline.
- You must be removed from the crew roster.
- Your character must be fully deleted and wiped from the server.

All Hard PKs must be agreed upon in roleplay. Discord agreements will not be recognized. A clipping recording of the agreement must be provided upon request from Gang Staff.

## Roster Rules

To maintain fairness and prevent roster manipulation, the following rules apply to all crews during active conflicts:

- Roster Lock: Once a race has been officially declared, crew rosters are locked and may not be modified until the conflict has concluded.
- Member Removal Cooldown: Any member removed from a crew is subject to a 10-day cooldown before they are eligible to rejoin the same crew.
- Roster Exploitation: Any attempt to exploit the roster system—such as repeatedly dropping and re-adding the same members to gain an advantage—is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action.

Roster rules are also specified in rules depending on the scenario that you are in. Please read all of the rules for clarification.

## Pink Slips

Pink slips are a crucial part of roleplay for the racing scene. Below will be listed the rules we have set in place for when pink slips are agreed upon.

- If you agree on a pink slip race, you are obligated to hold up your end of the deal immediately after the race(s) is finished. Scamming will NOT be allowed, and will result in disciplinary actions from staff.
- Requesting a pink slip race must have meaningful roleplay/context, you can not just go up to someone and say you want to race for that car.
- The whole interaction building up to the pink slip challenge, during and after need to be recorded.
- Anyone has the right to decline a pink slip challenge.
- The race(s) for the slip are to be agreed upon by the racers. If this can't be decided, they are to get a 3rd-party to choose the races.

## Turf Rules

Turf is to not be challenged, or messed with by a non-racing gang and vice versa

- **Claiming Turf:** Crews can claim as much turf as they want through roleplay, but will only be granted a 2-postal turf on the gang tablet.
- **Turf Interactions:** You can press anyone on your turf and tell them to get off. If they don't listen, you can challenge them to a pink slip and they have to agree OR pay your demands. (If this is found to be abused, staff will step in)
- **Turf challenging:** Turfs can be raced for/challenged until you are crew. You can not fight for turf of a crew or higher. Both crews must be trial crew or lower.

These structured guidelines ensure a fair and immersive roleplay experience for all participants. Always remember that **progression, promotions, and turf** are earned, not given. The better the roleplay, the greater the rewards.

# 🚦 Conflict Boundaries and Carryover

## Beef Carryover Rules

To ensure conflicts remain fair, contained, and event-specific, conflict actions may not carry over from the following scenarios:

- Official Crew Events
- Vendor Claiming

Any hostility, retaliation, or ongoing conflict stemming from the situations listed above must end once the event or claiming period concludes. Carrying over beef from these scenarios into general city RP is strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action.

## Robbing Rival Gang Members

Robbing is not allowed for racing crews


## Crew conflict & Escalation Rules

1. **Verbal Resolution — Words Come First**

    **All conflicts must begin with a genuine attempt at verbal resolution.**
- Crew leaders are required to make a good-faith effort to resolve disputes through an **in-person meeting or phone call.**
- Conflicts may **not** begin without clear **in-city reasoning and roleplay interaction.**
- Disrespect, ego clashes, and unresolved disputes are valid roleplay motivations, but they **must be expressed in character.**
- Examples of appropriate escalation:
    - “Let’s meet at the garage and talk this out.”
- Examples of inappropriate escalation:
    - “See you at your funeral.”


2. **Escalation Path - Your choice**

Once verbal attempts fail, crews may choose **Melee conflict and Racing or just skip to racing:**

**Melee Conflict**
 - If discussions break down and tensions rise, conflicts may escalate to melee-based confrontations.
 - Allowed weapons include fists, bats, and other melee weapons only
 - This phase is intended to allow resolution without permanent consequences.
     Examples include:
    - Gang-on-gang fights
    - Honor-based disputes
    - Organized brawls and body shots
 - Melee escalation should occur over a realistic duration and not be resolved in a single encounter. Players are encouraged to use this phase to develop roleplay.
- Some conflicts may be fully resolved through melee escalation alone.
 - In cases where melee escalation does not fully resolve the conflict, this does not grant permission to immediately escalate to firearms.
 - Even if a scene naturally escalates beyond melee, players must demonstrate believable roleplay, including fear, pressure, and a clear buildup that justifies further escalation.
- A brawl may end the conflict — or ignite a larger war.

 **Racing Escalation — Point of No Return**

- Escalation to crew vs crew racing is only permitted after:
    - Failed verbal resolution attempts, **and**
    - Completion of Verbal resolution
- Both crews must agree before racing escalation occurs.
- Crew leaders must conduct a meeting or phone call to formally declare a crew vs crew race
- Something must be at stake for both crews (e.g. Money, Materials, Cars, etc)
- Staff may intervene at any point if escalation is deemed invalid or unjustified.
- If all prior escalation attempts fail to resolve the conflict, firearm escalation may proceed — but only with strong, creative, and believable roleplay.
