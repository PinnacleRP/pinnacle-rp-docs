# 🧩 Business Guidelines

## Overview

The business system is here to create roleplay and keep the city active. Businesses should give players somewhere to go, something to do, and reasons to interact with each other. Making money is part of it, but it shouldn’t be the only goal.
Owning a business isn’t permanent. Staff may review businesses if they become inactive, cause ongoing problems, or stop contributing to the server.

## Ownership and Activity

Each business should have one main owner (possibly a Co-Owner) who is responsible for how the business runs. This includes staff behavior, activity levels, and making sure rules are being followed.
Businesses are expected to open and operate somewhat regularly. Nobody expects daily openings, but if a business disappears for long periods without notice it may be reviewed so the spot isn’t locked up. Staff will usually try to contact the owner before making any decisions where possible.

If you own a business, you are responsible for ensuring it is fully functional within the city. If required assets such as a suitable MLO (interior) or job-specific clothing/uniforms are not already available in the server, the owner is obligated to provide them.

## Roleplay Expectations

Businesses should create roleplay, not avoid it. Players should feel like they can walk in, interact, and get something out of the experience.
Running a business purely for income, avoiding interaction, or doing the bare minimum may lead to a review if it becomes a consistent issue.

## Owners and Employees

Owners are responsible for their employees while they are working under the business name. Internal issues should be handled by management first before involving staff unless server rules are being broken.
Employees must follow server rules while on duty and should only represent businesses they are actually employed by. Abuse of access, equipment, or position will be treated seriously.
Owners are also responsible for ensuring their business has appropriate uniforms, branding, and a proper working environment that fits the roleplay standard of the server.

## Economy and Competition

Pricing should stay reasonable and in line with the server economy. Trying to manipulate pricing, create artificial shortages, or damage the economy will result in staff stepping in.
Competition between businesses is fine and encouraged, but it needs to stay in roleplay. OOC targeting or attempts to shut down other businesses outside of RP isn’t acceptable.

## Events and Advertising

Businesses are encouraged to advertise and run events. Events should be organized responsibly and not intentionally disrupt other businesses or server performance.
Large events may need staff awareness depending on size.

## Clothing

Each business is allowed up to 8 pieces of clothing for free. If there is more needed to be put in, there will be a fee charged for each clothing item.

## Restrictions

The following is not allowed:
- Using business ownership to get around server rules
- Holding a business while inactive and preventing others from using it
- Using business resources for personal gain outside of roleplay
- Creating conflict outside of roleplay situations

## Staff intervention

Staff may step in if a business becomes inactive, repeatedly causes problems, negatively affects the economy, or receives consistent verified complaints.
This can result in warnings, changes to how the business operates, temporary closure, or ownership removal depending on the situation.

## Business Closure

Businesses may be closed if the owner steps down, becomes inactive, or the business no longer contributes to roleplay. Closed businesses may later reopen under new ownership if appropriate.

## Paycheck farming

Paycheck farming will not be tolerated. Employees are expected to actively roleplay and contribute while on duty. Being clocked in without participating, avoiding interaction, or being clocked on only to collect pay will be considered paycheck farming.

If you are caught:
- First Offence – Verbal/Documented Warning
- Second Offence – Final Warning
- Third Offence – Immediate removal of your employee role

If removed for paycheck farming, the role will not be reinstated

## One Business Per Organisation

Each organisation is limited to owning and operating one primary business at a time. This is to ensure fairness across the server and to prevent any single group from controlling multiple areas of the economy.

Organisations must choose which business they wish to represent. Attempting to run, manage, or indirectly control multiple businesses through alternate members, aliases, or proxy ownership will be treated as a breach of this guideline.
Members of an organisation may still work for other businesses as employees, but ownership and control must remain separate. Staff may review situations where overlap appears to bypass this rule.
If an organisation wishes to change businesses, they must step down from their current one before taking ownership of another and ownership can not fall to someone from there organisation.

## Menu Item Limits

Businesses are limited to a maximum of 8 food and/or drink items on their menu. This is to keep menus clear, balanced, and consistent across the server while encouraging quality roleplay over quantity.

Menus should remain fitting to the business theme and not be similar or duplicate style items to other businesses.
Any additions, removals, or changes to menu items must be approved by Human Resources and will be made at their discretion.

## Business Progression & Activity

Businesses that remain active, consistently open, and contribute to roleplay through regular operations, events, and community engagement may be eligible for upgrades or additional features.

This can include things such as expanded menu options, unique items,  or other business related benefits that enhance roleplay.
All upgrades and extras are not guaranteed and will be granted at the discretion of Human Resources based on overall activity, quality of roleplay, and positive contribution to the server.

Inactive businesses or those that fail to engage with the community should not expect progression or additional benefits.

## Final Note

The business system works best when owners stay active, fair, and focused on creating roleplay for others. The goal is to keep the city feeling alive rather than limiting opportunities.
